Making LLMs Show Their Reasoning Step-by-Step

What is Chain-of-Thought Prompting?

Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting is a technique where you ask an LLM to show its reasoning in intermediate steps before arriving at a final answer. Instead of jumping directly to the answer, the model explains its thinking.

Core Insight (Wei et al., 2022):
"Prompting the model to generate a chain of thought — a series of short thoughts that lead to the final answer — significantly improves performance on complex reasoning tasks."

The hypothesis: By forcing intermediate outputs, we constrain the LLM to engage in valid reasoning rather than pattern-matching shortcuts.

Few-Shot vs Zero-Shot CoT

Wei et al. showed two approaches:

Type How It Works Example
Few-Shot CoT Provide examples with reasoning steps, then ask question Show 2-3 examples with step-by-step solutions, then ask new problem
Zero-Shot CoT Just add "Let's think step by step" to any question No examples needed; simple magic phrase works!

Zero-Shot CoT: "Let's Think Step by Step"

One of the most surprising findings: simply adding "Let's think step by step" to a prompt dramatically improves accuracy, even without examples.

Medical Diagnosis Example

WITHOUT CoT prompt:

Prompt: "Patient: 65-year-old with chest pain, shortness of breath, and history of diabetes.
What is the most likely diagnosis?"

Response: "Likely diagnosis is a heart attack."

❌ Problem: No reasoning shown, could be wrong, hard to verify

WITH Zero-Shot CoT (just add one sentence):

Prompt: "Patient: 65-year-old with chest pain, shortness of breath, and history of diabetes.
What is the most likely diagnosis?

Let's think step by step:"

Response:
"Step 1: The patient has chest pain and shortness of breath.
These are classic symptoms of acute coronary syndrome.

Step 2: The patient has a history of diabetes.
This is a major risk factor for heart disease.

Step 3: Age is 65 (middle-aged).
This increases risk for MI.

Step 4: Considering all factors...
The most likely diagnosis is an acute myocardial infarction (heart attack).

However, we should also rule out:
- Pulmonary embolism (less likely without leg symptoms)
- Pneumonia (less likely without fever/cough)
- Aortic dissection (possible but less common)

Conclusion: Most likely = MI, but immediate ECG and troponin needed."

✓ Much better: reasoning is transparent, verifiable, more accurate

Why Chain-of-Thought Works

According to Wei et al. (2022), CoT works because:

  • Decomposition: Breaking complex problems into steps makes them easier to solve
  • Constrains reasoning: Intermediate outputs force valid reasoning, not shortcuts
  • Detects errors: Readers can spot mistakes in reasoning steps
  • Interpretability: We can see how the model arrived at conclusions
  • Compositionality: Combining simpler steps is easier than solving all at once

Performance Improvements (From Paper)

Wei et al. showed massive improvements on complex reasoning benchmarks:

Results from Wei et al. (2022)

Task (Benchmark) Without CoT With CoT Improvement
Arithmetic (GSM8K) 58.1% 90.9% +32.8% ⬆️

When CoT Helps (and When It Doesn't)

Task Type CoT Helps? Why
Multi-step math problems ✓ YES - Huge improvement Breaking into steps reveals structure
Reasoning/inference ✓ YES - Moderate improvement Makes logical steps explicit
Factual recall (who won the Nobel Prize?) ✗ NO - Minimal help Doesn't require reasoning, just memory
Classification (sentiment) ✓ SOMETIMES - Minor improvement Can help for edge cases, but simple tasks don't need it
Medical diagnosis ✓ YES - Very helpful Transparent reasoning critical for trust

Implementation: CoT in Medical AI

# Simple CoT implementation with Claude API
from anthropic import Anthropic

client = Anthropic()

def medical_diagnosis_with_cot(symptoms, medical_history):
    """Diagnose using Chain-of-Thought reasoning"""

    prompt = f"""
You are an experienced clinician analyzing a patient case.

Patient Information:
- Symptoms: {symptoms}
- Medical History: {medical_history}

Please diagnose this patient. Think step by step:

Step 1: Identify the key symptoms
Step 2: Consider differential diagnoses
Step 3: Evaluate risk factors
Step 4: Consider the most likely diagnosis
Step 5: What tests would you order?
Step 6: What is your final diagnosis and reasoning?

Take your time and reason through this carefully."""

    response = client.messages.create(
        model="claude-3-5-sonnet-20241022",
        max_tokens=1024,
        messages=[{"role": "user", "content": prompt}]
    )

    return response.content[0].text


# Example usage
symptoms = "Fever 101.5F, cough for 3 days, sore throat, no difficulty breathing"
history = "65-year-old, diabetic, hypertensive, smoker"

diagnosis = medical_diagnosis_with_cot(symptoms, history)
print(diagnosis)

# Output will show step-by-step reasoning, making it auditable and trustworthy

Limitations and Failure Modes

CoT Can Still Fail When:
• LLM lacks knowledge (reasoning steps don't help if you don't know the facts)
• Too many steps lead to drift (model forgets earlier reasoning)
• Complex multi-constraint problems confuse the step decomposition
• Domain requires expert knowledge steps can't express

Key Takeaways

  • Chain-of-Thought prompting = ask LLM to show reasoning steps before answering
  • Zero-Shot CoT = just add "Let's think step by step" (no examples needed!)
  • Huge improvements on math and complex reasoning (30%+ accuracy gains in paper)
  • Makes outputs auditable — you can verify the reasoning, not just the answer
  • Critical for medical AI — transparency builds trust
  • Doesn't help on simple tasks — only use CoT where multi-step reasoning is needed
